How to Keep a Machine Running Smooth

Using a sewing machine constantly without upkeeping it will make it break down eventually and will need to be fixed.
Lucky for you there are some things you can do to keep your machine running smoothly.
Written below are steps you can take to trouble shoot your machine. Troubleshooting is tryng different things to try to find the sourse of the problem and fix it.

Troubleshooting Steps:

  1. Clean it out - One of the most common things in slowing down a machine is lint sticking to gears. Get a clean brush or aircan and clean the lint from the inside of the machine.
  2. Oil the joints - Just as the Tin Man from "The Wizard of Oz" needed oil to get him moving, sewing machines need oil too. Purchase sewing machine oil and apply it to the inside moving parts. Careful not to put too much or lint wil cling to it and make a stick mess that will cause more damage.
  3. Change the needle - If a needle is bent or broken your seam is not going to happen. Also, make sure you have the right height. If you needle is the wrong height you'll most likely break it.
  4. Check your bobbin - A common mistake is having bobbins hat are the wrong size. If a bobbin is too small there will be no tension and you seam will look unbalanced.
  5. Search for loose screws - Grab a screwdriver and check all the screws to make sure none are loose or missing. Also, check for any wires that show some wear on them. Open wires are very dangerous!
